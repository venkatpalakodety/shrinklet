How Shrinklet works

Drag, choose a preset, compress, and save space — all privately on your Mac.

1

Drop files

Start from the large dashed drop zone and add PDFs, images, or a folder.

2

Choose preset

Use the Medium dropdown or switch to Light, Strong, Maximum, or Custom.

3

Compress

Review the pending queue and click Compress 1 File or Compress 7 Files.

4

Save or share

Open the compressed file, share it from the macOS share sheet, or remove it from the completed list.

Frequently asked questions

What files can Shrinklet compress?

PDF documents and images including JPG, PNG, TIFF, HEIC, GIF, and WebP.

Does Shrinklet compress videos or audio?

No. Shrinklet is intentionally focused on PDFs and images only.

What do the presets mean?

Light favors quality, Medium is balanced, Strong makes files smaller, and Maximum prioritizes the smallest output size.

Can I compress multiple files at once?

Yes. Add multiple PDFs and images, or add a folder, then compress them together.

Where are my files processed?

Shrinklet is designed for a local Mac workflow, so users do not need to upload files to a random website.

Is it hard to use?

No. The core flow is simple: add files, choose a preset, compress, and view the saved space.
