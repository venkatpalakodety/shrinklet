About Jungle Pixels

Small apps, thoughtful pixels, real productivity.

Jungle Pixels grew from everyday conversations with friends: the kind where someone says, “I wish there was an easier way to do this on my Mac.” Those chats sparked a passion for building focused apps that remove friction and help people get practical work done faster.

Built by Venkat, an indie maker who cares about useful, polished Mac software.

How Jungle Pixels came to be

The idea behind Jungle Pixels started with simple, honest conversations with friends, family, and fellow builders about the tiny workflow problems that slow people down. Compressing a PDF for an email, shrinking images before sharing, or preparing files for upload should not require confusing tools or random websites.

Shrinklet is the first expression of that mindset: a focused Mac app that does one thing clearly. It helps people make PDFs, images, videos, and audio files smaller, understand the result, and move on with their day.

Why “Jungle Pixels”?

The name reflects a mix of creativity and craft. “Jungle” feels alive, playful, and full of ideas. “Pixels” represents precision, design, and the small details that make software feel delightful.

The inspiration

The inspiration is productivity without friction. Jungle Pixels is about creating apps that are friendly, polished, and useful—not bloated, intimidating, or overloaded with features people do not need.

What Jungle Pixels believes

Focused toolsApps should solve clear problems without unnecessary clutter.
Respect for privacyEveryday workflows should avoid unnecessary uploads whenever possible.
Productive delightUseful software can still feel beautiful, calm, and enjoyable to use.

About Shrinklet

Shrinklet was created for people who need a fast, understandable way to compress PDF documents, image files, videos, and audio files on Mac. The flow is intentionally simple: add files, choose a preset, compress, review the result, and see how much space you saved.
